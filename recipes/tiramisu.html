Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert that is as elegant as it is delicious. Its layers of espresso-soaked ladyfingers and creamy mascarpone filling are topped off with a dusting of cocoa powder, creating a dessert that is as beautiful as it is flavorful.

This dessert is the perfect way to end any meal, as the combination of coffee and sweet cream make for a rich and satisfying flavor. The recipe is straightforward, yet the end result is a dessert that looks and tastes like it was made by a professional chef. It's no wonder that tiramisu has become a staple in Italian cuisine and is now enjoyed all over the world. Whether you're entertaining guests or treating yourself, tiramisu is the perfect dessert to impress and delight.

Ingredients

  • 6 large egg yolks
  • ¾ cup white sugar
  • ⅔ cup milk
  • 1 ¼ cups heavy cream
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pound mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • ¼ cup strong brewed coffee, at room temperature
  • 2 tablespoons rum
  • 2 (3 ounce) packages ladyfinger cookies
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder

Steps

  1. Whisk together egg yolks and sugar in a medium saucepan until well blended.
  2. Whisk in milk and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until mixture comes to a boil.
  3. Boil gently for 1 minute, then remove from the heat and allow to cool slightly.
  4. Cover tightly and chill in the refrigerator for 1 hour.
  5. Beat cream and vanilla in a medium bowl with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form.
  6. Remove egg yolk mixture from the refrigerator; add mascarpone cheese and whisk until smooth.
  7. Stir vanilla extract into the batter.
  8. Combine coffee and rum in a small bowl. Split ladyfingers in half lengthwise and drizzle with the coffee mixture. Arrange 1/2 of the soaked ladyfingers in the bottom of a 7x11-inch dish.
  9. Spread 1/2 of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, then spread 1/2 of the whipped cream over top. Repeat layers once more.
  10. Dust cocoa powder over top.
  11. Cover and refrigerate until set, 4 to 6 hours.
